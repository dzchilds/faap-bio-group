We use model-based approaches to understand population dynamics and natural selection in laboratory and free-living populations. We are particularly keen to understand how demographic and ecological processes interact to natural shape selection. Some general questions that interest us include: (1) Can we use evolutionary game theory (aka adaptive dynamics) to better understand selection on the life histories of free-living animals and plants, or are classical approaches sufficient? (2) How do temporal and spatial environmental variation shape the dynamics of populations in a changing environment? (3) Do we need to account for fluctuating selection to fully understand optimal life-histories in natural populations? For example, when does such variation select evolutionary bet-hedging strategies?
